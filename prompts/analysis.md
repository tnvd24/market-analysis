# Analysis prompt — paste a research pack below

Use this with a research pack generated by `asr pack build SYMBOL --format md`.
It runs on your Claude subscription in a chat window; no API key, no cost beyond the plan.

The pack contains **only computed facts** — prices and indicators a library calculated from
stored rows, rule-based signals with the numbers that triggered them, and news quoted
verbatim. Nothing in it has been interpreted. Interpretation is what this prompt is for.

---

## The prompt

> You are helping me research an Indian equity. Below is a **research pack**: deterministic
> data computed from stored market data — I have not interpreted any of it, and neither
> should you beyond what the numbers support.
>
> **Ground rules:**
> 1. **Use only what is in the pack.** Do not add prices, ratios, fundamentals, or events
>    from memory — your training data is stale and this stock's numbers are right here.
> 2. **Cite the numbers.** Every claim you make must point at a specific value or headline
>    from the pack. If you say momentum is weakening, say which indicator says so.
> 3. **Refuse rather than guess.** If the pack lacks what you'd need (a null indicator, no
>    news in the window, too little history), say so plainly. Do not fill the gap.
> 4. **Read the data-quality section first.** If it flags an unadjusted price jump or stale
>    data, say what that invalidates *before* discussing anything downstream of it.
> 5. **No price targets, no buy/sell calls.** This is research I read, not advice I act on.
>
> **Give me:**
> - **What the data says** — the technical picture, in plain English, with the numbers behind it.
> - **What's driving it** — do the filings/headlines explain what the price is doing? Say so
>   only if the pack supports the link; "no visible catalyst" is a valid, useful answer.
> - **Tension and disagreement** — where do the signals contradict each other? (e.g. oversold
>   RSI while below the 200-day: is this a dip or a downtrend?) This is the most valuable
>   part; do not smooth it over into a tidy story.
> - **What to watch** — the specific, checkable things that would confirm or kill the current
>   read. Name the level, the date, or the filing.
> - **What you'd need that isn't here** — the gaps. Be concrete.
>
> Be direct and short. Skip the preamble.

---

## Variations worth keeping

**Compare several stocks** — paste multiple packs and add:
> Rank these by the strength of the technical setup, and be explicit that this ranks the
> *evidence*, not the investment. Where two stocks look similar, say what would separate them.

**Follow-up on one stock** — paste yesterday's read and today's pack:
> Here is my previous read and today's updated pack. What actually changed, and does it
> break the earlier reasoning? Do not restate what is unchanged.

**Stress-test a view** — after a first read:
> Argue the opposite case using only the pack. What in this data would a bear (or bull) point
> to that we just walked past?

---

## Why it is shaped this way

The rules above are not politeness — they are the guardrails that keep the two-track design
honest. The automated track never lets a model touch the data (no hallucinated prices), and
this prompt never lets the model reason past it (no invented context). The model's job is
narrative and framing over numbers it did not produce, and cannot change.

Research only. Not investment advice.
